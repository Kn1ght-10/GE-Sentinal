# Merch clans and coordinated pumps — sample corpus note

"Merching" groups coordinate to accumulate an item quietly, promote it in
chat channels and social platforms, ride the resulting demand spike upward,
and then dump holdings on followers. The market fingerprint is a sustained
multi-hour ramp on abnormally high two-sided volume, followed by a retrace
toward the pre-event price once distribution finishes.

Grand Exchange buy limits per 4-hour window add friction to accumulation, so
coordinated groups spread buying across many accounts and across time. That
is why volume z-scores against a per-item rolling baseline, rather than raw
volume, are the more reliable tell.

The discriminating contrast with legitimate demand (a content-creator video,
a new training method) is the retrace: organic repricing tends to persist,
while pump-and-dump cycles round-trip.
