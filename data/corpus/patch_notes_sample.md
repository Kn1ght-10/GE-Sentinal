# Game update repricing — sample corpus note (illustrative entries)

Game updates legitimately reprice items: a combat rebalance can permanently
raise demand for one weapon and sink another; a new boss can introduce supply
of a previously scarce drop; a quality-of-life change can shift consumable
usage rates. These moves arrive on high volume and DO NOT retrace — they are
the textbook benign look-alike for a pump.

Illustrative (fictional, for retrieval testing): "Combat beta: the speed of
the example whip-class weapons increased by one tick" — expect a step-change
in price and a multi-hour volume surge for affected items immediately after
the update window.

GE-Sentinel treats announced update timestamps as a suppression window and a
negative labeling function: anomalies inside the window are down-weighted and
the memo agent is required to surface the update as a benign explanation.
